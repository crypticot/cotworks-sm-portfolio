Community Strategy

TechiesMatch: The Wired Community

Problem Statement

TechiesMatch needed a way to build trust and familiarity with its target audience before asking them to sign up for a matchmaking platform. Cold acquisition for a trust-based product is difficult. People do not join a dating or networking app because they saw an ad — they join because they already feel like they belong somewhere connected to it. The product had no community infrastructure and no warm audience to convert from.

Approach

Designed a community-led growth strategy built around a dual-platform architecture. Rather than picking one channel, the strategy assigned distinct roles to each platform: Telegram for daily conversation and peer engagement, LinkedIn for professional visibility and broader audience acquisition. The community was given a distinct identity — The Wired — with its own positioning, rules, and reason to exist beyond just promoting the product. Every activity in the calendar was tied to a specific goal so the community never felt like a waiting room for a sales pitch.

Deliverables

  • Community concept and positioning statement
  • Target audience and member profile
  • Platform strategy for Telegram and LinkedIn with defined roles for each
  • 2-week community activity calendar across both platforms
  • 10 discussion prompts tailored to the TechiesMatch audience
  • Community rules and moderation guidelines
  • Community-to-product conversion plan
  • Growth and retention strategy

Outcomes

Evaluation Highlight

Submitted as part of Stage 7 of the HNG Cohort 14 Sales and Marketing track. The dual-platform structure and conversion strategy were specifically noted by evaluators as intentional and product-aligned. The community concept directly informed the Telegram channel used in the TechiesMatch website launch.

Key Learnings

A community only works when it has a reason to exist that is separate from the product it supports. The most important design decision here was giving The Wired its own identity. Members join for the community and stay for the product — not the other way around. Platform selection also matters more than most strategies acknowledge: the roles each platform plays need to be distinct, otherwise you are just posting the same thing in two places.

Brevo Email Automation

TechiesMatch: Email Funnel Campaign

Problem Statement

TechiesMatch needed an automated email system that could move a cold lead from first contact to product sign-up without requiring manual follow-up at every step. The challenge with a trust-based matchmaking platform is that people need more than one touchpoint before they commit. A single welcome email is not enough. The system needed to do the work of building familiarity, handling objections, and nudging action — all without feeling robotic.

Approach

Mapped the full customer journey from awareness to retention before writing a single email. Defined three audience segments with different entry points and different objections, then built the automation logic around behaviour rather than time alone. If a user clicked, they moved forward. If they did not open, the sequence adjusted. The email copy was written to feel human first — direct, specific to the TechiesMatch audience, and focused on what the reader was thinking at each stage rather than what the product wanted to say.

Deliverables

  • Full funnel map across five stages: awareness, interest, consideration, conversion, and retention
  • Three audience segments with defined pain points, objections, and messaging direction
  • 7-email sequence with subject lines, preview texts, body copy, and CTAs for each email
  • Automation workflow with entry triggers, time delays, branching logic, and behaviour-based conditions
  • Personalisation strategy covering name, segment, behaviour, and use-case-based messaging
  • Screenshots of emails built and sent inside Brevo
  • KPI framework covering open rate, click-through rate, conversion rate, and re-engagement rate

Outcomes

Performance Metrics

Received a 96.7% rating as one of the strongest strategy documents by mentor evaluators. Specifically praised for good subject lines.

Key Learnings

Email marketing fails when it is written from the brand's perspective instead of the reader's. Every email in this sequence was written by asking: what is this person thinking right now, and what do they need to hear to take one step forward? The branching logic was also a significant upgrade over a linear sequence — it meant the automation responded to real behaviour instead of assuming everyone moves at the same pace.

Website & SEO

TechiesMatch: SEO Website

Problem Statement

TechiesMatch needed a public-facing website that could do three jobs at once: explain the product clearly to someone who had never heard of it, rank for relevant search terms to bring in organic traffic, and convert visitors into waitlist sign-ups or app downloads. Most early-stage startup websites do one of these well and ignore the others. The brief also required a minimum of eight distinct pages, WCAG accessibility compliance, and a documented SEO strategy.

Approach

Started with keyword research before writing a single line of copy. Used Ubersuggest with location set to All Locations rather than Nigeria, because Nigerian search volume is systematically underrepresented in aggregated tools. Mapped keywords to search intent and assigned each page a primary conversion goal before designing the content structure. Every page was built around the principle that it should provide value before asking the visitor to take action. The Safety page was added as a trust-building measure specific to the matchmaking context — no other submission in the cohort included one.

Deliverables

  • Live multi-page website at techiesmatch.netlify.app
  • Keyword research report with search volumes, difficulty scores, and intent mapping
  • SEO implementation across meta titles, descriptions, heading hierarchy, image alt text, and URL structure
  • Conversion-focused copy across all pages including a waitlist funnel
  • Safety page addressing the specific trust concerns of a matchmaking audience
  • WCAG accessibility compliance across contrast, readability, alt text, and keyboard navigation
  • Full strategy document explaining page selection, keyword decisions, and CTA placement rationale

Outcomes

Performance Metrics

Received an 86% rating as one of the best reviewed works for SEO and content strategy by mentor evaluators. The keyword research insight was specifically cited as a sophisticated research decision.

Key Learnings

SEO without conversion thinking is just traffic. The most useful insight from this project was that every page decision — what to include, where to place the CTA, how to structure the FAQ — should be made with both the search engine and the human reader in mind at the same time. The Safety page was also a reminder that for trust-based products, removing fear is a more powerful conversion lever than listing features.

Founder-Led Marketing Strategy

Michael Abu: MenuCard Founder Strategy

Problem Statement

MenuCard, a digital menu creation platform, needed to build awareness and credibility in a market where most potential users had never considered switching from physical menus or generic design tools. Paid acquisition at an early stage is expensive and short-lived. The founder had a story and a point of view that the product alone could not communicate. The challenge was to turn that into a sustained content presence that built trust before asking anyone to try the product.

Approach

Positioned the founder rather than the product as the primary voice. Defined a clear founder narrative built around a specific problem the founder observed, why it mattered, and why he was the right person to talk about it. Built content pillars that gave the founder consistent topics to post about without repeating himself, then designed a 4-week calendar across Instagram, Twitter, and LinkedIn with platform-specific formats for each. The engagement strategy focused on converting content viewers into product users through DMs, comment responses, and community participation rather than direct calls to sign up.

Deliverables

  • Founder positioning statement and narrative
  • Three content pillars with rationale and example post ideas for each
  • 4-week content calendar across Instagram, Twitter, and LinkedIn
  • Platform-specific post formats tailored to each audience
  • Engagement and conversion strategy covering daily actions, DM approach, and sign-up path
  • WCAG accessibility considerations for all content formats
  • Mock-ups of content across all three platforms

Outcomes

Performance Metrics

Received a 10.5/10 score, one of the only submissions in the cohort to receive extra credit. Described as exceptional and noted it thought outside the brief in a way others did not.

Key Learnings

Founder-led marketing only works when the founder has a genuine point of view, not just product updates. The most important decision in this project was building the content pillars around the problem space rather than the product features. A founder who talks about the problem their audience faces every day builds more trust in a month than a year of product announcements.

Campaign Strategy

The Boring Product Campaign

Problem Statement

The challenge was to take a low-interest, everyday product with no obvious emotional appeal and build a complete marketing strategy around it that could realistically drive growth from zero. Most commodity products fail at marketing because they lead with features or price. The task was to demonstrate that strong positioning and a clear customer understanding can create demand for almost anything.

Approach

Started by identifying who would actually care about the product and why, before touching any copy or creative. Built a customer persona grounded in a specific use case rather than demographics alone. The positioning strategy focused on one outcome the target customer wanted, then built the entire funnel around delivering that outcome in every touchpoint — from the first ad impression to the post-purchase email. The landing page was designed to convert a visitor who knew nothing about the product into someone ready to buy within a single scroll.

Deliverables

  • Full-funnel marketing strategy from awareness to action
  • One detailed customer persona with pain points, motivations, and objections
  • 3 ad copies across different formats
  • 2 ad poster creatives
  • Landing page copy and structure
  • Email flow covering welcome, nurture, and conversion
  • 3 growth hack ideas grounded in the product's specific context

Outcomes

Strategic Core

Demonstrated the ability to build a complete marketing system for a product with no inherent marketing advantage and established the foundational funnel approach carried into later stages.

Key Learnings

Every product has a customer who genuinely needs it. The work is finding that person and speaking directly to them instead of trying to appeal to everyone. This project also reinforced that a landing page is not a brochure — it is a conversation with a specific person who has a specific doubt, and every section exists to resolve one of those doubts before the next one appears.

Multi-Channel Strategy

Comprehensive Marketing Strategy: MenuCard

Problem Statement

MenuCard needed a marketing strategy grounded in real data rather than assumptions. As a digital menu creation platform, it sat in a space where most potential users either did not know the product existed or did not yet see a reason to switch from how they currently handled menus. The strategy needed to identify where those users were, what they were searching for, and how to reach them across multiple channels without spreading a limited early-stage budget too thin.

Approach

Started with a direct conversation with the founder to understand the product, the target audience, and the competitive landscape before forming any strategic opinions. Used that conversation to anchor all subsequent research. Keyword research was conducted to map what potential users were actually searching for, then search intent was used to assign each keyword to the appropriate channel and funnel stage. The multi-channel plan was built so that each channel reinforced the others rather than operating independently.

Deliverables

  • Founder engagement summary and key product insights
  • Keyword research report with search volumes, intent categories, and channel assignments
  • Multi-channel strategy covering SEO, paid ads, social media, content marketing, and email
  • Channel-specific recommendations with rationale for each
  • Content direction for blog, social, and educational formats
  • Full strategy presentation deck

Outcomes

Strategic Core

Keyword research and intent mapping directly informed the more advanced SEO work done for future products in later phases.

Key Learnings

A marketing strategy built without talking to the product owner is guesswork dressed up as analysis. The founder conversation in this project changed several assumptions that would have produced a weaker strategy. It also reinforced that multi-channel does not mean doing everything — it means choosing the channels where your specific audience already is and making sure they work together.

Crisis PR

Crisis PR & Brand Management Simulation

Problem Statement

TechiesMatch, as a matchmaking platform, carries an inherent trust risk. Any safety incident involving a user — whether real or alleged — has the potential to destroy the platform's credibility overnight, particularly in a market where trust is the primary product. The challenge was to design a crisis response system that could protect the brand's reputation, stabilise public sentiment, and rebuild user trust after a serious safety-related incident became public.

Approach

Defined a realistic crisis scenario specific to the matchmaking context before building any response materials. The scenario was grounded in the product's actual vulnerability rather than a generic crisis template. Stakeholder groups were mapped individually because each group needed different information, delivered through different channels, in a different tone and at a different time. The recovery campaign was designed to address the root fear the crisis had created — that the platform was not safe — rather than just managing the news cycle.

Deliverables

  • Full crisis definition including what happened, who was affected, why it mattered, and public risk metrics
  • Formal press release draft
  • Three public-facing social media response posts
  • Stakeholder communication plan covering customers, employees, media, investors, and brand ambassadors
  • Brand recovery campaign with metrics, messaging, content ideas, and execution timeline
  • KPI framework for measuring brand recovery across sentiment, retention, and engagement metrics

Outcomes

Performance Metrics

Received a rating of 88.9%, praised for exceptional crisis clarity with vivid, specific scenario details. Stakeholder differentiation and recovery approach described as highly sophisticated.

Key Learnings

Crisis communication is not about controlling the narrative — it is about being the most honest voice in the room before someone else defines the story for you. The most important structural decision in this project was treating each stakeholder group as having a completely different emotional state and information need. Precision in stakeholder mapping is the difference between a response that holds and one that creates a second crisis.

B2B Growth

Enterprise B2B Growth Architecture

Problem Statement

A mid-stage SaaS company with an AI-powered HR analytics product was struggling to convert leads into paying customers. The sales team complained that marketing leads were not ready to buy. Deal cycles were long with high drop-off after demos. The company was also attempting to expand from Nigeria into the UK with limited brand awareness in the new market. The budget was $5,000 per month, the sales team had four reps, and the marketing team had two people. The task was to build a complete B2B growth system that worked within those constraints.

Approach

Rejected a linear funnel model from the start because enterprise B2B deals do not work that way. Multiple people inside the buying company have different priorities and different objections, and they rarely move together. Built the strategy around a multi-threaded buyer journey that mapped three distinct roles in the buying committee — their conflicting priorities, hidden objections, and what each one needed to say yes. Demand strategy was split into creation and capture, with separate channel logic for Nigeria and the UK because the two markets required different messaging approaches and had different levels of product awareness.

Deliverables

  • Multi-threaded buyer journey across three buying committee roles
  • Demand creation and demand capture strategy with channel and messaging rationale for both Nigeria and UK markets
  • Content ecosystem including one flagship asset, three supporting assets, and two sales assist assets
  • Lead scoring model combining behavioural and firmographic signals with custom MQL and SQL definitions
  • Paid acquisition plan with channel allocation, CAC expectations, and risk analysis across a $5,000 monthly budget
  • Non-linear email funnel with branching logic for engaged non-converters, demo no-shows, and post-demo drop-offs
  • Sales and marketing SLA with feedback loop system
  • Outbound sequences across LinkedIn and email for three distinct buying roles
  • Objection handling matrix, demo narrative structure, and deal rescue playbook

Outcomes

Strategic Value

The constraint-based thinking — particularly the paid acquisition model under a fixed budget — directly informed the prioritisation approach used in later projects.

Key Learnings

B2B growth fails when marketing treats the company as the buyer instead of the committee. Every decision in this architecture was made by asking which person inside the organisation it was actually for. The other significant learning was about constraint as a creative tool — a $5,000 budget forces prioritisation decisions that a larger budget never would, and those decisions produce a more coherent strategy than one that tries to be everywhere at once.
